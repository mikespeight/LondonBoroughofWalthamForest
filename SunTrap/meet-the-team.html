Meet the team

Our Staff

  • Kerry Rolison

    Head of Suntrap

    Kerry is a qualified primary school teacher. Before joining Suntrap, she had four years teaching in schools in Haringey and Waltham Forest. She joined Suntrap as a bright eyed naturalist in 1989. Since then, she has inspired thousands of young people with her knowledge and enthusiasm for the outdoors. She became Head in 2005 and has since introduced a successful family learning programme.

  • Emma Lewis

    Senior Tutor

    Emma is a qualified secondary Science teacher with experience working in schools in Sussex. She joined our team in 2012, from a Field Centre in Buckinghamshire..

  • Louise Horsfall

    Tutor

    Louise joined us in 2016 bringing her experience as an outdoor and environmental educator in the local area. She is a qualified Forest Schools practioner who firmly believes in the power of experiential learning and outdoor contexts to engage and inspire learners...

  • Joe Ahvee

    Tutor Resources Manager

    Joe joined our team in 2016 to take on a new role as both a tutor and managing our grounds as a teaching resource. He is currently working on a project to create a meadow on the edge of our camping field.

The Animals

  • Cornsnake

    Cornflake the corn snake is our friendly resident born in 2005. This smooth lady is often found in the corn fields of central and north America..

  • Salamanders

    Our resident fire salamanders are fascinating amphibians which are naturally found under rocks, logs or holes in the forests of Europe and Africa..

  • Chickens

    We have a variety of chickens at Suntrap. In the evenings they roost in their pens, during the day they have free range of the Suntrap gardens.

  • Giant African Snails

    Our giant African land snails have no bones, no legs but can get around! From damp areas in Africa, these snails feast on plants and collect pieces of rocks to help them make their shells..

  • Rabbits

    The Suntrap rabbits arrived in 2005 and we’re named by our visitors on our open day. Chip and Pin are furry bodied, warm blooded herbivores who love a cuddle.

  • Indian stick insects

    Indian Stick Insects are great at hide and seek as they can use camouflage to hide from predators. It eats leaves on trees and bramble leaves in the forests in India.

  • European common toad

    The European common toad protects themselves by puffing up to look bigger than and can produce a nasty taste from their glands. It livse in damp places in Europe, NW Africa and Asia. .

  • Madagascan hissing cockroach

    The Madagascan hissing cockroach is a friendly and endearing creature. Found on the forest floor in the Madagascan island off the East Coast of Africa.

  • Cold water and tropical fish

    At Suntrap there are both cold water and tropical fish. Originally from the East, they’re now kept as our lovely pets here at the centre..

  • Tortoise

    Our tortoise, Sasha is still young but loves an adventure in the long grass.

  • Bearded dragon

    Norbett is our resident bearded dragon. Although scaly in skin he’s gentle in character and loves a stroke.

  • Bees!

    Buzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzzz. Kids. They will sting you. You better be fast runners!
