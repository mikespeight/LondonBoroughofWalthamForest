Developing a respect for the natural world. Intrinsic in our activities is that everyone learns to enjoy, value and respect Epping Forest to care for it into the future. Suntrap staff will share learning objectives at the start of an activity. Learning will be assessed throughout the session using a range of strategies including: different types of questioning, peer or self assessment or discussion in groups or pairs.

Forest art

Our Forest art activity is inspired by the work of landscape artist Andy Goldsworthy who uses natural material such as flowers, icicles, leaves, mud, pinecones, snow, stone, twigs and thornes to create temporary sculptures which draw out the character of their environment. After looking at examples, in the forest, small groups will create their own work of art using the natural world as their canvas. This activity links to the national curriculum alongside Art and Design and PSHE guidance. 

Bushcraft skills

Small groups work together to collect wood and bring it back to the Suntrap grounds. Here, in a contained , safe environment they will build their own fire and light it using different methods. If they master the task, they get rewarded with toast and jam as toasted themselves on their fire. This activity links to the national curriculum alongside PSHE guidance in the areas of health and wellbeing, relationships and living in the wider world.
