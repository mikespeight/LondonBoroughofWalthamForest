October newsletter

Today's the day

Today Walthamstow Wetlands opens its gates to the wider public for the very first time in 150 years! All are welcome here at Europe’s largest urban wetland where admission is free, seven days a week - 363 days of the year. Located just a mere 10 minutes’ walk from Blackhorse Road tube station, London’s newest nature reserve provides a unique haven to explore, enjoy and connect with your better nature.

Relax and unwind

Built in 1894, the Victorian Engine House is a locally-listed building. It has now been refurbished to house the visitor centre, cafe, gallery, viewing terrace and educational space. Why not spend a morning or afternoon relaxing in the comfy chairs of the Engine House Cafe where you can sample delicious food or even just a freshly-brewed warm drink surrounded by stunning views?

Get involved

Wetland habitats are extremely rare in urban areas. In collaboration with Thames Water and London Wildlife Trust, the project has already enriched existing areas as well as created entirely new ones. You can get involved by helping us care for the habitats, by assisting as a guide on various tours and workshops or as a welcome host in the visitor centre. So step away from the urban jungle and enjoy some fresh air with us here at Walthamstow Wetlands!

Upcoming events

Family fun

21st - 29th October
There are a number of fun, family-friendly half term activities taking place throughout next week. From nature-themed storytelling and arts & crafts to minibeast tracking and mammal home-building workshops, there is something for children of all ages and interests.
We will have lots more events coming up over the next few weeks and months so keep an eye out on our website to join us for fun at Walthamstow Wetlands.
